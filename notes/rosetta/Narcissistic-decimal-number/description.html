A   http://mathworld.wolfram.com/NarcissisticNumber.html Narcissistic decimal number   is a non-negative integer,   n,   that is equal to the sum of the   m-th   powers of each of the digits in the decimal representation of   n,   where   m   is the number of digits in the decimal representation of   n. Narcissistic (decimal) numbers are sometimes called   Armstrong   numbers, named after Michael F. Armstrong.
They are also known as   Plus Perfect   numbers.
An example: ::::*   if   n   is   153 ::::*   then   m,   (the number of decimal digits)   is   3 ::::*   we have   13 + 53 + 33   =   1 + 125 + 27   =   153 ::::*   and so   153   is a narcissistic decimal number
