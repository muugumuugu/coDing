These define three classifications of positive integers based on their   Proper divisors|proper divisors. Let   P(n)   be the sum of the proper divisors of   n'   where the proper divisors are all positive divisors of   'n'   other than   'n   itself. if P(n) < n then n' is classed as 'deficient (https://oeis.org/A005100 OEIS A005100). if P(n) == n then n' is classed as 'perfect (https://oeis.org/A000396 OEIS A000396). if P(n) > n then n' is classed as 'abundant (https://oeis.org/A005101 OEIS A005101).
Example: 6'   has proper divisors of   '1',   '2',   and   '3. 1 + 2 + 3 = 6',   so   '6   is classed as a perfect number.
Task: Calculate how many of the integers   1'   to   '20,000   (inclusive) are in each of the three classes. Show the results here.
