wp:Heron's formula|Hero's formula for the area of a triangle given the length of its three sides   a,   b,   and   c   is given by: :::: A = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}, where   s   is half the perimeter of the triangle; that is
:::: s=\frac{a+b+c}{2}.
http://www.had2know.com/academics/heronian-triangles-generator-calculator.html Heronian triangles are triangles whose sides and area are all integers. : An example is the triangle with sides   3, 4, 5'   whose area is   '6'   (and whose perimeter is   '12).
Note that any triangle whose sides are all an integer multiple of   3, 4, 5';   such as   '6, 8, 10,   will also be a Heronian triangle. Define a Primitive Heronian triangle as a Heronian triangle where the greatest common divisor of all three sides is   1   (unity). This will exclude, for example, triangle   6, 8, 10.
