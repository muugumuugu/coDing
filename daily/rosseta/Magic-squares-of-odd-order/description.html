A magic square is an   NxN   square matrix whose numbers (usually integers) consist of consecutive numbers arranged so that the sum of each row and column,   and   both long (main) diagonals are equal to the same sum (which is called the   magic number   or   magic constant). The numbers are usually (but not always) the first   N2   positive integers.
